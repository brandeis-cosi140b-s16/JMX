b'Mesut \xc3\x96zil a surprise inclusion on shortlist for Fifa world XI midfield'


b'<p>Arsenal\xe2\x80\x99s Mesut \xc3\x96zil, Chelsea\xe2\x80\x99s Cesc F\xc3\xa0bregas and Eden Hazard, Manchester City\xe2\x80\x99s Yaya Tour\xc3\xa9 and \xc3\x81ngel Di Mar\xc3\xada of Manchester United are among 15 nominees for the three-man midfield in the 2014 Fifa FifPro world XI.</p> <p>With largely indifferent club form, and having missed a large part of the year through injury, Ozil is seen as a surprise nomination for the world XI, but joins other World Cup winners Toni Kroos and Bastian Schweinsteiger on the list, which also includes four candidates from Spain: Xabi Alonso, F\xc3\xa0bregas, Xavi Hern\xc3\xa1ndez and Andr\xc3\xa9s Iniesta.</p> <p>The 15 shortlisted midfielders were chosen by professional players in a poll organized by FifPro, the international group of players\xe2\x80\x99 unions which has more than 65,000 members.</p> <p>Arturo Vidal joins Juventus team-mates Andrea Pirlo and Paul Pogba on the list while five of the 15 played for Champions League winners Real Madrid this year, including Di Maria, who alongside James Rodr\xc3\xadguez of Colombia and Vidal of Chile are the only South Americans nominated. Tour\xc3\xa9 of the Ivory Coast is the only African and there is no Asian player.</p> <p>Nominations for the goalkeeper spot were announced on Monday, with Real Madrid\xe2\x80\x99s Iker Casillas a surprise inclusion, and the final XI will be named at the Ballon d\xe2\x80\x99Or ceremony on 12th January 2015 in Zurich.</p> Fifa FifPro World XI nominated midfielders: <p>Xabi Alonso (Spain, Bayern Munich); \xc3\x81ngel Di Mar\xc3\xada (Argentina, Manchester United); Cesc F\xc3\xa0bregas (Spain, Chelsea); Eden Hazard (Belgium, Chelsea); Xavi Hern\xc3\xa1ndez (Spain, Barcelona); Andr\xc3\xa9s Iniesta (Spain, Barcelona); Toni Kroos (Germany, Real Madrid); Luka Modric (Croatia, Real Madrid); Mesut \xc3\x96zil (Germany, Arsenal); Andrea Pirlo (Italy, Juventus); Paul Pogba (France, Juventus); James Rodr\xc3\xadguez (Colombia, Real Madrid); Bastian Schweinsteiger (Germany, Bayern Munich); Yaya Tour\xc3\xa9 (Ivory Coast, Manchester City); Arturo Vidal (Chile, Juventus).</p>'
