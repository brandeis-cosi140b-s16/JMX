b'Bayern Munich sign 10-year kit deal with Adidas worth reported \xe2\x82\xac900m'


b'<p>Bayern Munich have extended their kit deal with Adidas until 2030 for a reported \xe2\x82\xac900m (\xc2\xa3645m). The contract had another five years to go but the German kit manufacturer moved early to secure a further 10 years with the newly crowned Bundesliga champions.</p> <p>\xe2\x80\x9cWith this extension of the contract until 2030 Adidas will continue its long partnership with FC Bayern M\xc3\xbcnchen,\xe2\x80\x9d said Adidas spokesman Oliver Br\xc3\xbcggen.</p> <p>The announcement came shortly before Bayern were beaten by Borussia Dortmund in the German Cup semi-final on Tuesday night, missing all their penalties in the shootout to lose 2-0 after the game was tied 1-1 in extra time. The sportswear firm owns 8.33% of Bayern and Br\xc3\xbcggen added they would \xe2\x80\x9cin the future continue to work on co-operations with top teams and federations in international football\xe2\x80\x9d.</p> <p>The figure is well below the \xc2\xa3750m deal Adidas signed with Manchester United in July 2014, but is worth more than Arsenal\xe2\x80\x99s \xc2\xa330m-a-year deal with Puma that started at the beginning of this season.</p> <p>United\xe2\x80\x99s agreement with the company commences at the start of the 2015-16 season and ends Nike\xe2\x80\x99s 13-year relationship with the club, although United will still be wearing their Nike shirts during their summer tour of the US. A club statement at the time said: \xe2\x80\x9cManchester United has reached a 10-year agreement with Adidas for a global sponsorship and dual-branded licensing deal for a minimum guarantee of \xc2\xa3750m, subject to certain adjustments, beginning with the 2015-2016 campaign.\xe2\x80\x9d</p>'
