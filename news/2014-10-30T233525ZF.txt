b'Five English players on 40-man shortlist for Golden Boy award'


b'<p>Five English players are on the 40-strong shortlist to be crowned as Europe\xe2\x80\x99s best young player.</p> <p>While Gareth Bale was the only British player to make Fifa\xe2\x80\x99s 23-man shortlist for the Ballon d\xe2\x80\x99Or award this week, the nominations for this year\xe2\x80\x99s prestigious Golden Boy award, which was first established by the Italian newspaper Tuttosport, features Liverpool\xe2\x80\x99s Raheem Sterling, Calum Chambers of Arsenal, Luke Shaw of Manchester United, Everton\xe2\x80\x99s John Stones and Eric Dier of Tottenham.</p> <p>A whole host of Premier League-based players have also made the shortlist including the Chelsea defender Kurt Zouma, Liverpool winger Lazar Markovic and Manchester United forward Adnan Januzaj.</p> <p>The award, open to any players aged 21 and under, is voted for by journalists from 30 newspapers around the world and Wayne Rooney remains the only English winner after picking up the prize in 2004. The France and Juventus midfielder Paul Pogba is the holder having finished just ahead of Everton\xe2\x80\x99s Romelu Lukaku last year.</p> <p>Other previous winners include Lionel Messi (2005), Sergio Ag\xc3\xbcero (2007) and Mario G\xc3\xb6tze (2011).</p> The full shortlist: <p>Premier League Calum Chambers (Arsenal), Kurt Zouma (Chelsea), John Stones (Everton), Raheem Sterling, Lazar Markovic, Divock Origi (Liverpool), Adnan Januzaj, Luke Shaw (Manchester United), Nabil Bentaleb, Eric Dier (Tottenham).</p> <p>La Liga Gerard Deulofeu (Sevilla), Aymeric Laporte (Athletic Bilbao), Sandro Ramirez, Munir El Haddai (Barcelona), Sa\xc3\xbal \xc3\x91\xc3\xadguez (Atl\xc3\xa9tico Madrid).</p> <p>Serie A Domenico Berardi (Sassuolo), Bernardeschi Franco (Fiorentina), Kingsley Coman (Juventus), Keita Bald\xc3\xa9 Diao (Lazio), Mateo Kovacic (Inter).</p> <p>Bundesliga Hakan Calhanoglu (Bayer Leverkusen), Pierre-Emile Hojbjerg, Gianluca Gaudino (Bayern Munich), Matthias Ginter (Borussia Dortmund), Julian Green (Hamburg), Maximilian Meyer (Schalke).</p> <p>Ligue 1 Marquinhos, Adrien Rabiot (Paris Saint-Germain), Benjamin Mendy (Marseille), Lucas Ocampos (Monaco).</p> <p>Eredivisie Tonny Vilhena (Feyenoord), Jetro Willems (PSV), Arkadiusz Milik (Ajax).</p> <p>Jupiler League Dennis Praet, Alexander Mitrovic (Anderlecht).</p> <p>Others Oliver Torres (Porto), Bruma (Galatasaray), Gonzalez (Basel), Nikola Ninkovic (Partizan Belgrade), Luka Zahovic (Maribor).</p>'
